When to See a Doctor

You should consult a dermatologist or healthcare provider immediately if:

  • You notice any new, changing, or unusual moles or skin lesions
  • A lesion bleeds, itches, or becomes painful
  • You have a family history of skin cancer
  • You have had significant sun exposure or sunburns
  • A mole shows asymmetry, irregular borders, color variation, or diameter greater than 6mm (ABCDE rule)
  • You have any concerns about your skin health
  • This tool suggests a potentially concerning result

Early detection through professional screening saves lives. Regular check-ups with a dermatologist are essential, especially for high-risk individuals.

Limitations of the AI Model

While our deep learning model has been trained on thousands of images, it has important limitations:

  • Not 100% Accurate: Even with high accuracy rates, the model can produce false positives and false negatives
  • Image Quality Dependent: Poor lighting, blurry images, or incorrect angles can significantly affect results
  • Limited Training Data: The model is trained on specific datasets that may not represent all skin types, lesion presentations, or rare conditions
  • No Context Awareness: The AI cannot consider your medical history, symptoms, family history, or other clinical factors
  • Skin Type Bias: Performance may vary across different skin tones and ethnicities due to dataset limitations
  • Rare Conditions: The model may not accurately identify rare or unusual skin conditions not well-represented in training data
  • Non-Cancerous Mimics: Benign conditions can sometimes resemble malignant lesions, and vice versa
